<p><p>Cell separation and characterization in solid tumors involve various techniques tailored for isolating specific cell populations. Fluorescence-Activated Cell Sorting (FACS) uses fluorescent markers to sort cells based on emitted light. Magnet-Activated Cell Sorting employs magnetic fields to separate cells tagged with magnetic beads. Density Gradient Centrifugation utilizes differential sedimentation based on cell density. Dielectrophoretic methods exploit electric fields to separate cells by their dielectric properties. Aqueous Two-Phase System relies on phase separation in a biocompatible environment, while Field Flow Fractionation separates cells based on size and shape. Each method is vital for cancer research and therapy development.</p></p>
