<p><p>Cloud computing platforms can be categorized into two main types: cloud-based and on-premises. Cloud-based solutions utilize remote servers to store, manage, and process data, offering scalability, flexibility, and cost-efficiency, as businesses can access services over the internet without heavy infrastructure investment. On-premises software, on the other hand, involves deploying applications and systems locally within a company's own data centers. This option provides greater control and security but often requires higher initial costs and ongoing maintenance efforts.</p></p>
